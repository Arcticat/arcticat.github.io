---
title: "Why I Love Messy Datasets"
date: 2026-06-28
description: "Clean data teaches you tools. Messy data teaches you judgment."
tags: ["data", "opinion"]
---

Everyone celebrates clean datasets, but the messy ones are where the real
learning happens.

A messy dataset forces you to ask questions a tutorial never will: Why are
there three different spellings of the same city? What happened in March
that made half the values disappear? Is that outlier a sensor glitch or the
most interesting row in the whole table?

Cleaning data isn't the boring part before the analysis. Half the time, it
*is* the analysis.
