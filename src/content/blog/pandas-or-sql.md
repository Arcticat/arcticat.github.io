title: "Pandas or SQL? Wrong Question"
date: 2026-07-14
description: "The two tools I reach for most, and why 'which is better' misses the point."
tags: ["data", "python"]
---

Every few months the same debate resurfaces: should analysts learn pandas
or SQL? After years of using both daily, my answer is that the question
itself is broken.

SQL is how you talk to data where it lives. Pandas is how you shape data
once it's yours. One pulls the right rows out of a billion; the other turns
those rows into something a human can reason about.

Learn SQL to ask good questions. Learn pandas to explore the answers.
Fluency in the handoff between them — knowing *when* to leave the database —
is the actual skill.
